Abstract:

In this work, a survey was carried out to identify the current European landscape from grid and market stakeholders point of view and to improve the coordination between the Transmission System Operators (TSOs) and Distribution System Operators (DSOs). The survey includes two parts, one dedicated to grids tools and services and other to the markets tools and services. This survey aims to identify exists tools and services in order to adapt them to the future challenges. Based on the survey results, it was identified that major concerns of the energy stakeholders are related with the integration of different platforms used for different purposes (for example, operational planning and real time systems), and how to exchange information/data between parties in an expedited and standardized way. The survey participants see energy storage as the most essential technology in the future energy system, followed by the smart metering, online voltage regulation and demand response services. However, on the market landscape, survey results suggest that the creation of limited number of fully integrated regulated markets is the most consensual solution to avoid the fragmentation in many different markets and products.

I. Introduction

The identification of the existing tools used by grid and market stakeholders to provide their services are essential to improve the coordination between the Transmission System Operators (TSOs) and Distribution System Operators (DSOs). The coordination between TSOs and DSOs is necessary to improve the optimal and reliable utilisation of Distributed Energy Resources (DERs) as well as secure and economic operation of the entire power system [1], [2]. These DERs could provide system services when coordinated and managed in an intelligent manner. Moreover, demand response resources such as, electric vehicles (EVs) [3], heat pumps and energy storage connected to the distribution could provide significant benefits in terms of systems flexibility [1].
